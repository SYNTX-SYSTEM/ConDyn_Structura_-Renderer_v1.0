8. RISK ASSESSMENT

The development of a deterministic structural compute layer introduces non-trivial architectural and institutional risks.

These risks are explicitly acknowledged and structurally mitigated.

Technical Risks

Risk Description Mitigation Strategy
State Explosion Unbounded growth of structural graph complexity Version pruning logic and bounded constraint domains
Constraint Overfitting Excessive rigidity limiting system adaptability Hierarchical constraint layering with scoped overrides
Replay Latency Performance degradation under full state reconstruction Incremental state hashing and delta compression

Institutional Risks

Risk Description Mitigation Strategy
Adoption Resistance Institutional hesitation toward structural governance change Pilot-limited integration and phased exposure
Governance Misalignment Conflict between existing policy frameworks and constraint logic Configurable axiom registry and external audit validation
Regulatory Interpretation Risk Ambiguity in classification of structural decision systems Early regulatory consultation and compliance documentation

Execution Risks

Risk Description Mitigation Strategy
Scope Drift Expansion beyond deterministic validation objectives Stage-gated milestone enforcement
Resource Concentration High dependency on core architectural contributors Structured documentation and reproducible build pipelines

Risk is treated as an architectural variable, not an externality.

All mitigation strategies are embedded in system design rather than deferred to operational practice.
